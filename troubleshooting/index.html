Charging issues §

Car won't charge overnight: Most common issue - car goes to sleep, charger thinks it's not there.

  • Fix 1: Schedule via car app (not charger app), leave charger unlocked
  • See our Alpine A290 charging guide for setup tips and cold-weather advice
  • Fix 2: Use Octopus Go (not Intelligent) - more reliable
  • Fix 3 (Zappi): Lock/unlock car, flash headlights off/on to reset
  • Fix 4 (Ohme): Ohme can apply "CP wake up fix" - ring their support
  • Red light at charge port = charge failed/stopped

Zappi + IOG: Works for some ("We have 2 A290s, both charge perfectly with Zappi and IOG using charger integration since July"). Fails for others - intermittent charging interruptions. Software update promised but reports suggest it doesn't fully fix the issue.

Deep sleep charge failure: If you plug in many hours before the scheduled charge (e.g. 7–8 hours early), the car can enter deep sleep and the charger fails to wake it. Fix: Plug in closer to the scheduled charge time — 1–2 hours before is ideal. This is separate from the IOG issue above. If you are choosing hardware, the working home charger list is a useful starting point.

Octopus IOG "Charge to add" trap: If you set "Charge to add" in the Octopus app, set the target to 90%+ (not lower). The car's own 80% charge limit overrides Octopus, so if Octopus thinks it only needs to add 20% it may not bother starting a session. Setting it high ensures Octopus always initiates the charge.

Occasional charge failure: Sometimes the car just doesn't charge even when programmed. Requires manual override. App/communication glitch.

Range showing wrong after charge: Charge to 90%, shows 121 miles (195 km), reset kW/mile → shoots to 179 miles (288 km). The "guess-o-meter" is based on recent driving history. Reset the trip computer for a more realistic figure - or just ignore range entirely and use battery percentage.

App problems §

Complete app disconnect: All data disappeared from app. Usually a Renault server issue. Resolves itself (hours to days). "The app gave me option to reconnect the car" after server fix.

Charge % display lag: App shows battery level 3–4 hours behind actual during charging. Known issue, no fix.

"Subscription deactivated" message: Message in car's 4G network status. Usually a server glitch even though the subscription shows active.

Android login loop: Known issue Jan 2026 after Renault backend changes. Fixed by app update.

Radio no sound bug: Radio shows channel but no sound. Fix: Say "Hey Google" or quick press the microphone button on the wheel - when the system gets bored listening, normal volume returns. For more infotainment tips, see dashboard setup.

Software & electrical §

"Check Lights" warning: Appeared once, possibly auto lights related. Fix: switch to manual lights - went away next journey.

Dashboard/infotainment reboot: Screens went black and rebooted twice during a trip. Rare occurrence, usually resolves itself.

Speed limiter default on: Speed limiter selected as default every startup (annoying but no limit set). Press button to right of steering wheel twice to turn off.

Telemetrics not loading: "Error loading app" message. If GT model: it's an optional extra (not standard). If GTS/GT Performance: should be standard, may need app update.

Eco score popup: Pops up when switching off, shows 0/100. Says "press for more info" - press it and it disappears. Renault OS leaking through Alpine skin.

Mechanical issues §

Rear brake disc corrosion: Heavy rust on rear discs due to regen braking. Occasionally drive with regen off to force use of physical brakes. Not a fault - normal for EVs with heavy regen.

Driveshaft rust: Reports of driveshafts rusting within weeks. Concern especially for coastal owners. Underbody treatments (Lanoguard) mentioned as protection. Owners looking at protection products often start with the styling & accessories section.

Suspension creak: Creaking noise over rough roads. Multiple reports, no solution from Alpine yet.

Front rattle: Rattling from driver's front side over bumps. Multiple dealer visits; some had suspension and brake parts replaced. One solution: subframe bolts tightening.

Whistling heater: Whistling at fan levels 3–4. Related to pollen filter. Dealer can fix.

Paint & body §

Matt Grey Tornado maintenance: Use matte specific cleaner only (PH neutral). Products for shiny paint have fillers that ruin matte finish. Smart repairs are harder - dings and scrapes stand out more. PPF highly recommended.

Leather seat deformation (GTS): Driver's seat develops two indents that don't smooth out. Alpine is aware of a "significant number of owners" experiencing this. Sourcing new leather supplier. Will inspect and replace leather/seat base at no cost once new materials are available. If buying again, some would choose GT Performance cloth seats instead.

Wheel bolt corrosion: Wheel nuts rust/corrode quickly. 17mm black wheel bolt covers as first mod (almost universal). Car comes with locking wheel bolts "frustratingly in black!" See the best first mods for the Alpine A290 if you want an easy cosmetic fix.
