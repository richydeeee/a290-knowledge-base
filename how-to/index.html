Charge efficiently §

Home charging (overnight):

  • £1.50–£2.30 per charge (10–20% to 80%) on cheap overnight rates
  • 7kW charger: 3–4 hours for a full charge
  • Octopus Go: Simple cheap overnight rate, works with any charger
  • Octopus Intelligent Go: Cheaper rate but requires a compatible charger. Works with Ohme (select Alpine as car model in the app). Some owners report issues with MyAlpine app integration, but Ohme handles scheduling independently

Need charger ideas before you book an install? See the confirmed Alpine A290 home chargers.

Public charging:

  • Cheapest: Tesla Superchargers (39p/kWh, accessible to non-Teslas via app)
  • Octopus Electroverse app: Highly recommended - order the RFID card
  • Mobilize charge pass: £4.99/month gives 43p/kWh at Ionity (vs c.90p otherwise)
  • 100kW DC rapid charger: c.30 mins for 15-80% (the A290 maxes out at 100kW)

Fast charging in cold weather: Cold battery charges VERY slowly initially (13kW reported at 2°C, 40% SoC). Warm battery charges much faster (38–66kW at 9°C). Pre condition by driving hard before arriving at a charger. Temperature makes a 5x difference to charging speed. If your car still refuses to start a timed session, check the charging troubleshooting guide.

Charging to 100% - the debate §

Many charge to 100% regularly on lease/PCP. Common approach: 80% daily, 100% for long trips.

Note: No regen at 100% for the first few miles (km) - some compromise at 90%. Many switched to a 90% limit in winter due to reduced range. For more real-world charging behaviour, see common charging issues.

Maximise range §

Driving technique (counterintuitive!): Drive harder to warm the battery = better range than gentle driving.

Regen strategy: High in town, low on fast roads, increase for downhill. Gets 4 mi/kWh when optimised vs 3.1 when driven like ICE.

Temperature management: Pre heat car before trips (on charger power, not battery). Use heated seats and steering wheel rather than cabin heating. Winter: 2.8–3 mi/kWh vs summer: 3.4–3.7 mi/kWh.

Speed impact:

  • At 60–65mph: 150–175 miles (240–280 km) possible
  • At c.74mph: 3.1 mi/kWh
  • At 70mph motorway: range "depletes rapidly" vs urban

If you are new to the car, the new owner guide covers the basics before you start chasing efficiency.

Short journey tip: First few miles are incredibly inefficient (battery not warmed). A 1-mile (1.6 km) trip gets only 1 mi/kWh; a 26-mile (42 km) trip gets 3 mi/kWh once warmed.

Use hidden features §

  • Reset consumption display: Hold down button on right side of steering wheel for 3 seconds
  • Steering wheel controls: Press up/down arrow to scroll through dashboard displays
  • Hold drive mode button: Shortcuts to customise that mode's settings and sounds
  • Parking brake tip: Hold parking brake switch up longer than a click = brakes harder (useful on steep hills)
  • Key fob trick: Naff plastic key comes apart - allows small lanyard attachment for garage key
  • Instrument cluster: Car icon matches your actual car colour
  • AC quirk: AC turns off when you flick to Auto (known bug)

If you have just collected the car, the new owner guide is the best companion to these Alpine A290 hidden features.

Set up the dashboard §

Turn off Google speed alerts: Go to Google navigation in main infotainment (not via CarPlay). Set 'alerts off'. Also press furthest right button by driver's door twice to disable overspeed warning + lane departure.

Brightness adjustment: Can only adjust the day setting during the day, night setting at night when active.

Telemetrics: Standard on GTS/GT Performance. Optional extra on GT. If yours will not load correctly, see software & electrical troubleshooting.
