Buying decisions & pricing §

Salary sacrifice/leasing is a popular route via Octopus EV — "Everything covered: insurance, maintenance, tyres, tax." GT on salary sacrifice: ~£211/month net (3yr, 10k miles/year). GTS+ typically £300–370/month. Leasing often £350/month + VAT, 12k miles, 36 months.

Finance: MY25 models at 2.9% APR; MY26+ at 4.9% APR. 1.9% APR deals available at some dealers on GTS models. Cash buyer discounts of up to £5,000 reported; finance discounts up to £3,000 at 1.9%.

Government grant: MY26+ models eligible for the full £3,750 Electric Car Grant (previously £1,500 for non-"+" models). The £40k threshold matters for luxury car tax — some have managed to spec dealer cars under £40k.

Stock vs factory order: Stock cars available with ~4 month delivery. Lots of stock reported — Autotrader listings increased from 55 to 75 in one month. Many come with driver packs people don't want. Some dealers had 20–30+ cars on site.

Model differences §

GT (180PS): Base model, 180bhp. No Devialet sound system (pedestrian warning sound only). No Nappa leather. Standard cloth seats. Telemetrics optional extra.

GT Performance (220PS): 220bhp (same as GTS). Cloth seats — some owners prefer these to leather for durability. Devialet and telemetrics optional or standard depending on spec.

GT Premium (DISCONTINUED in MY26): Was the "sweet spot" — 180PS with Nappa leather and Devialet. No longer available.

GTS (220PS): 220bhp. Nappa leather seats, Devialet sound system, blue Brembo calipers all standard. GTS Premiere Edition available with all-blue interior and special badging. Floor mats included. Telemetrics standard.

MY26+ changes §

  • Full £3,750 government grant (vs £1,500 on MY25)
  • One-pedal driving feature (not available on earlier models, no confirmed retrofit)
  • White with black roof now a no-cost option
  • Black now a £700 paid option
  • APR increased from 2.9% to 4.9%
  • Finance ~£20/month increase
  • GT Premium model discontinued
  • Nappa leather and Devialet now only standard on GTS+, cost options on GT+/GT Performance+

Range reality (set expectations!) §

WLTP is 226 miles (GTS). Real-world figures are significantly different:

  • Summer (15°C+): 180–210 miles
  • Winter (0–5°C): 100–130 miles
  • Motorway winter: Can be as low as 100–115 miles (confirmed by Renault/Alpine technical bulletin)

EVs universally lose 30–40% range in cold weather. The small battery (52kWh usable) means drops are felt more acutely. Not ideal as an only car for long-distance commuters. Perfect as a second car or for short commutes.

"I travel 52 miles each way a few times a week (all motorway) but no charger at the other end. Will I make it there and back in the GTS?" — "Risky. You'll be cutting it fine in winter."

"Having owned two French electric cars and with a GTS on order, I strongly suggest: use a small piece of black sticky tape to cover the predicted range on the screen. It is a complete nonsense. Use the battery percentage."

Insurance §

Dramatically cheaper than comparable ICE hot hatches. Typical range: £200–300/year for experienced drivers with NCB.

  • Aviva: "A little over £200 a year"
  • Admiral multicar: ~50% less than comparable ICE (A35 AMG comparison)

Common first impressions §

"Haven't had that much fun in a hot hatch since the R5 GT Turbo."

"Gets looked at just as much as my previous Astons but is much more suitable for pottering around London."

"I have had more positive comments about my A290 than any other car I've owned."

"Handling though, crikey, cocks its wheel like a p155ing dog."

"Interior feels like a £70k car."

"The sound system is awesome for a 36k car / blows the socks off 99% of other manufacturers."

Common concerns: Range anxiety (especially winter), no cup holders, poor reverse camera quality, app reliability, torque steer (though "nowhere near as prevalent as reviewers lead you to believe").

Buy this car if… §

  • You want a fun, engaging hot hatch with EV instant torque
  • You can charge at home overnight cheaply
  • Your daily commute is <50 miles round trip
  • You have another car or don't mind charging stops for long trips
  • You appreciate interior quality and sound system excellence
  • You want something that gets attention and compliments

"I was very EV hesitant if I am honest, but initial outlay aside, the numbers make sense."

"After owning two Puma STs on the bounce, I was sceptical going the EV route but may be hard to return as I'm besotted."

Think twice if… §

  • You need 200+ mile motorway range in winter as your only car
  • You can't charge at home
  • You regularly do 100+ mile commutes without charging access
  • You need rear seat space for adults regularly
  • You want a "set and forget" reliable app experience
  • You hate the idea of no cup holders
