Buying decisions & pricing §

Salary sacrifice/leasing is a popular route via Octopus EV - "Everything covered: insurance, maintenance, tyres, tax." GT on salary sacrifice: c.£211/month net (3yr, 10k miles/year). GTS+ typically £300–370/month. Leasing often £350/month + VAT, 12k miles, 36 months.

Finance: MY25 models at 2.9% APR; MY26+ at 4.9% APR. 1.9% APR deals available at some dealers on GTS models. Cash buyer discounts of up to £5,000 reported; finance discounts up to £3,000 at 1.9%.

Government grant: MY26+ models eligible for the full £3,750 Electric Car Grant (previously £1,500 for non-"+" models). The £40k threshold matters for luxury car tax - some have managed to spec dealer cars under £40k.

Stock vs factory order: Stock cars available with c.4 month delivery. Lots of stock reported - Autotrader listings increased from 55 to 75 in one month. Many come with driver packs people don't want. Some dealers had 20–30+ cars on site.

Model differences §

GT (180PS): Base model, 180bhp. No Devialet sound system (pedestrian warning sound only). No Nappa leather. Standard cloth seats. Telemetrics optional extra.

GT Performance (220PS): 220bhp (same as GTS). Cloth seats - some owners prefer these to leather for durability. Devialet and telemetrics optional or standard depending on spec.

GT Premium: The "sweet spot" for many owners. 180PS with Nappa leather, Devialet sound system, heated steering wheel and blue brake calipers. From £34,500.

GTS (220PS): 220bhp. Nappa leather seats, Devialet sound system, Brembo calipers (red as standard — blue is an option, not standard). GTS Premiere Edition available with all blue interior and special badging. Floor mats included. Telemetrics standard.

MY26+ changes §

  • Full £3,750 government grant (vs £1,500 on MY25)
  • One-pedal driving feature (not available on earlier models, no confirmed retrofit)
  • White with black roof now a no cost option
  • Black now a £700 paid option
  • APR increased from 2.9% to 4.9%
  • Finance c.£20/month increase
  • A290+ range introduced as a refreshed evolution of the lineup
  • Nappa leather and Devialet now only standard on GTS+, cost options on GT+/GT Performance+

Range reality (set expectations!) §

WLTP is 226 miles (364 km) (GTS). Real-world figures are significantly different:

  • Summer (15°C+): 180–210 miles (290–340 km)
  • Winter (0–5°C): 100–130 miles (160–210 km)
  • Motorway winter: Can be as low as 100–115 miles (160–185 km) (confirmed by Renault/Alpine technical bulletin)

EVs universally lose 30–40% range in cold weather. The small battery (52kWh usable) means drops are felt more acutely. For detailed range estimates by speed and conditions, see the EVKX range and consumption calculator.

Common first impressions §

Common concerns: Range anxiety (especially winter), no cup holders, poor reverse camera quality, app reliability, torque steer (though "nowhere near as prevalent as reviewers lead you to believe").

Buy this car if… §

  • You want a fun, engaging hot hatch with EV instant torque
  • You can charge at home overnight cheaply
  • Your daily commute is <100 miles (160 km) round trip
  • You have another car or don't mind charging stops for long trips
  • You appreciate interior quality and sound system excellence
  • You want something that gets attention and compliments

Think twice if… §

  • You need 200+ mile (320+ km) motorway range in winter as your only car
  • You regularly do 100+ mile (160+ km) commutes without charging access
  • You need rear seat space for adults regularly
  • You want a "set and forget" reliable app experience
  • You hate the idea of no cup holders
