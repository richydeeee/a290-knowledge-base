Delivery checks §

  • Ensure blue wheel centre caps are installed (if specced) — may be in the glove box
  • Check locking wheel nuts are aligned with valve stems and logos
  • Tyre pressures often set to 2.6bar (ECO) at delivery — causes harsh ride. Recommended: 33–35psi (from ~40psi delivery) reduces torque steer and improves ride
  • PPF recommended if ordered — some dealers install pre-delivery

App setup §

The My Alpine app is required but "buggy." Complete setup in the car first before attempting app connection.

  • Android login loop issue (Jan 2026) — fixed by app update
  • Kelec app available as backup (more reliable but fewer functions)
  • App often lags 3–4 hours behind actual battery level during charging
  • Server outages common — complete app disconnects happen

Charging setup (critical!) §

Do NOT overcomplicate. Use normal Octopus Go (not Intelligent Octopus Go) for reliability. Set the charge schedule in the car's app (e.g. 00:30–05:30), not in the charger app. Leave the charger unlocked.

"The more integrated you try to make the setup (IOG + car + charger), the more likely it is to fail."

The car has a ~0.1kW trickle to keep awake while plugged in.

Ohme workaround: Download the separate Octopus app, link the car through that, disable scheduled charging in the Alpine app.

Software update (late 2025): Alpine confirmed a fix for Intelligent Octopus Go incompatibility, but R5 owners report it "still doesn't intelligent charge" after the update. Zappi + IOG works for some, not others.

First-week tips §

Disable lane-keep assist: Press far-right button by driver's door TWICE (shows yellow 'my perso' system off). Also disables overspeed warning. Resets every ignition cycle — must do each time.

Speed camera alerts: Go to Google navigation settings in main infotainment (not CarPlay) → set 'alerts off'. Can't fully silence — still bongs even when muted.

Seat quirks: Heated seat turns off if nobody sitting in it after 5 seconds. Heated seat/wheel don't work below 40% battery for pre-heating.

Drive settings:

  • OV (Overtake) button: Red button on steering wheel = temporary power boost
  • Hold drive mode button for a couple seconds = shortcuts to customise mode settings
  • "My personal" mode trick: Everything sport except 'engine' set to eco = A/C in eco, limits top speed ~80mph, gives 3.7+ mi/kWh

Pedestrian warning sound §

Can be changed in drive settings. The 'Alpine signature' is "not very pleasant and rather loud." The 'Neutral' sound is much more pleasing.

Common new-owner issues §

Rear brakes seizing: Rear brakes bind after washing or sitting in rain/cold — regen means physical brakes are rarely used. Fix: drive briefly after washing, or occasionally drive with regen off.

Torque steer: Most say "nowhere near as prevalent as reviewers lead you to believe." Often confused with lane-keep assist pulling the wheel. Setting tyres to 33–35psi significantly reduces it.

Dashboard quirks: Eco score pops up when switching off (shows 0/100) — Renault OS leaking through Alpine skin. No equaliser adjustment on sound system. Heated seat switch appears in LHD position on screen.

Electronic hum: Normal high-pitched whining from front console/firewall when car is awake. DC-DC converter noise — not a fault.
